    Welcome my GitHub Page

    This is my personal-professional page. I'm a developer in Chrome and work mostly in video related topics, more concretely in WebRtc related things. I have a mixed background in signal processing, embedded/Real-Time systems, and basically all kinds of software plumbings.

    Life at Google, Chromium and WebRtc

    I've spent the best part of three years now in those overlapping places and, phew, it has been quite a ride. I started in Stockholm and about half the way moved to Mountain View. I'm proud of a few things I've done and less proud I didn't have the time or wasn't speedy enough to get a few others done (this category is mostly related to VP8/9 encoding/decoding and Zero Copy capture). Basically I divided my life between doing Blink-related features and plumbing Video Capture and the Real-Time video processing in Chrome in all platforms.

    Blink-wise I'm quite happy with the MediaStream Recording API implementation. This was a long standing effort ascribed to the WebRtc realms for the simple reason that it records the Web Platform live media abstraction: MediaStream. Quite notably, the original bug tracker was the most starred ever in Chromium yet it had been dangling with no one really having the time to commit to it. This work was mostly concentrated in the second half of 2015. As a bonus, I landed/am still landing the stream capture from <video>/<audio> elements.

    Under the hood I'm one of the OWNERS of code areas related to video capture and GetUserMedia/MediaStream implementation, which basically translates into the folders media/capture, content/renderer/media and content/browser/renderer_host/media (although following the usual lifecycle of any Chromium committer, I have done stints in other parts of the code, e.g. third_party). A few notable things I've work in are e.g.

    • landing the AVFoundation support for Mac capture and device monitoring; this was a humongous effort because Chrome ships a single binary for Mac and AVFoundation had to be detected and loaded on runtime, which gave way to tons of bugs and OS glitches.
    • supporting Android Camera 2.0;
    • wiring the Capture Device capabilities to the renderer;
    • connecting Flicker detection based on timezone;
    • supporting the Tango 3D cameras;
    • porting OpenCV for PNaCl;
    • Chromium Code Search is a neat Chrome extension to search in the Cr codebase :-}
    • taking over a mojo "blob" for video capture, basically bundling together all video capture related code into an execution entity delimited by Mojo boundaries. Quite cool if you ask me.
    • Wrote a cryptic wiki entry on video capture

    Life before all that

    Yeah I did do interesting stuff before joining G (I guess previous employers wouldn't be thrilled if I was to give too many details so I'll keep it fuzzy). My previous employer was Alcatel-Lucent-Bell Labs in Antwerpen, Belgium, same place were (mostly) the same guys developed and shipped a bazillion DSL boxes giving service to a ton of homes all around the world. In particular I worked in the DSL aggregator IP (the whole thing is marketed as ISAM) which is the secret sauce of where all DSL or PON customer lines get bundled into a massive chunk of uplinks of about ~500GBps (usually splitted into a number of 10/40 or even 100GB ethernet over fiber). ALu is a great place to do network equipment and, well, not too good a place for anything else.

    Also in Belgium (with trips to Noordwijk in the Netherlands) I worked for this contractor of the European Space Agency that got me to deal with two systems; one was a VR for astronaut experiments, measuring the response time to different audiovisual stimuli, where I did the real time parts (basically a pace maker sucking managing FIFOs for sensor inputs and actuator outputs, all using RTX for Win - sig).  The other project was an attitude and heading tracking to support astronauts onboard the ISS (this one eventually made it up there!); in here I did the Kalman filter formulation for all sensor integration (those days accelerometers and gyros were not integrated and was quite a challenge to solder them, let alone model it).

    Immediately before that I worked in Spain for a few years in Real Time Linux systems for trains, more concretely for train-ground communication and a bit on train controllers. That was a ton of fun, dealing with those tiny RTAIs (later Xenomais) bridging GRPS and GSMR to the MVB (a Real-Time serial bus on the train from where all systems hang, including engines and brakes!). Moreover it's heart warming to see those systems in operation all around the world carrying tiny pieces of me :)

    Even before that I finished a M.Sc. in CompSci in UCD (Ireland) on power consumption estimation for software running on embedded devices, which was a quite interesting research that lead to a few publications here and there.
